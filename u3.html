MODERN ENGLISH AUSTRALIA 4 ways to say "U" at the beginning of English words

Part 1 – Identify the Sound

Lesson: Listening and speaking are connected. Notice how your mouth, lips, tongue, and jaw move. Some sounds come from airflow only; others use the vocal cords. Click the short videos below to hear each sound once with pronunciation.

/juː/ — “you” sound (like university)

The lips start rounded, then spread slightly as the tongue moves from high back to front. Air flows smoothly as the voice continues.

/uː/ — long “oo” sound (like oops)

The tongue is high and back, and the lips are rounded and pushed forward. The sound is voiced and continuous.

/ʌ/ — short “uh” sound (like uncle)

The tongue is in a relaxed mid-central position, lips are neutral, and the jaw is slightly open. The sound is short and natural.

/ɜː/ — “er” sound (like urgent)

The tongue is mid-central with lips slightly rounded, and the vocal cords vibrate to produce a long, controlled sound.
